Break-even is a revenue pace target — "are we earning enough each month to cover our costs?" It tells you if the business model is working at current volume.

P&L net income is whether you're actually making money this period. It can differ from break-even because of timing corrections, non-cash items (depreciation), or one-time charges. You can be above break-even pace but still report a loss.

Cash is when money physically moves. We can be profitable on paper while cash is tight — this happens when wholesale customers are slow to pay (AR lag), or when a big outflow hits that isn't spread evenly across the month.

Which one to use: Revenue pace → "should we push harder on sales this week?" · P&L → "is the business structurally profitable?" · Cash trough → "do we need to accelerate collections or delay a purchase?"
